# Give Way Rules

直行 > 左转 > 右转

Give way > Stop

The basic give way rules are:

1. Give way to your **right** at **roundabouts** (and all other situations)

2. Give way to vehicles coming from **straight** ahead if you are **turning right**

3. Give way to vehicles **turning left** if you are **turning right**

4. Give way to all vehicles on the top of a **T-intersection** if you are on the bottom of the T

5. Give way to all vehicles moving **uphill** if you are moving **downhill **

6. When coming to a pedestrian crossing with a **raised traffic island in the middle**, you only need to stop if the pedestrian is crossing or waiting to cross your side of the crossing.

   If the crossing **doesn't have a central raised traffic island**, then you must stop when the pedestrian is waiting to cross on your side of the road, is crossing your side of the road, or is on the crossing on the other side of the road walking towards your side

   **Courtesy crossings** are usually made of bricks or paving and are often raised above the level of the road. They are not official pedestrian crossings, but they do provide a place where drivers can stop safely to allow pedestrians to cross.



- A driveway is treated like an uncontrolled intersection.
- If you are turning at an intersection, give way to all vehicles not turning.
- If you deviate from the centre line, generally you have to give way
- At a stop sign, you have the least priority and all other vehicles have the right of way before you, unless they are at stop signs, too, in which case the give way rules apply
- A police officer's directions overrule any give way or stop signs.



## Roundabout

The clockwise nature of a roundabout means you give way to vehicles coming from your right. Technically, if someone is using a roundabout incorrectly they might come from your left (and you should avoid hitting them), but this situation would be almost unheard of. It's likely they would be at fault in any incident.

  Turning the wheels **towards the kerb** means if the handbrake/parking brake fails the car will come to rest against the kerb and won't roll any further. 

  Putting it in **reverse gear** will provide a lot of engine braking.

- If someone can move the vehicle immediately you may park on or closer than 50cm to a fire hydrant.
